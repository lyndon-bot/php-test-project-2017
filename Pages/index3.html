    How to Record a Video
    There are several methods to create and record a reference video or about me video, and most desktop and laptop computers are already equipped with a web camera that allows you to do so. To record a video, you can use one of many free programs: QuickTime®, iMovie®, Debut®, and Apowersoft® are just a few that allows you to record and save videos to upload to your i. am page. While a desktop computer provides a more stable foundation, a laptop can easily be fixed on a solid platform, to make a video recording. Some programs are designed for a PC setup, others for a Mac, and some are available for both PC and Mac work environments. NOTE: The suggested formats are recommendations only, and inquireaboutme.com is not in partnership with QuickTime®, iMovie®, Debut®, and Apowersoft®.

    Video Requests
    The accepted formats to upload videos to your i. am page are: .m4v, .mpg, .avi, and .wmv. These are the formats needed to upload ‘About Me’ or ‘Accolades’ videos, as well as ‘Reference Videos’.

    Other Formats
    For i. am pages ‘About Me’ that will use Powerpoint® documents, the accepted formats are .mov, and .pptx. Animation and commercial videos should be saved in the following formats: .m4v, .mpg, and wmv. Ideal opportunity to post professional videos that are customized, and highlight your skill set. For all formats, simply browse to the localized area on your hard drive, and upload to the respective area.

    Creating the Ideal Recording Environment
    The area that is viewed in the web camera live space, should be clean and an office set up is ideal. The area should be well-lit, with no additional movement in the background, or sound (other than your voice). Your attire should be professional, as though you’re conducting a Skype® interview. Practice before recording, and only upload a solid presentation, with no breaks in conversation. Your video should be short, with the ideal recording time being 1 to 4 minutes (no more than 5 minutes). Eye contact should remain entirely on the web camera, reading from notes is not ideal, and speaking fluently (no pauses) makes your statement look more genuine. To end your video, have your hand and mouse ready to stop recording, eliminating the need to look away from the web camera. Remember, the goal is to present yourself in the best professional manner possible. This information should be shared to those that will serve as a reference for you as well.
